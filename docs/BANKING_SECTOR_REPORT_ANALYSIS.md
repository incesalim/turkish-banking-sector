## 2. ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORK

### Four-Pillar Analysis Structure

#### A. Credit Analysis
**Metrics Tracked:**
- Overall loan growth rate (28% in report example)
- Growth momentum indicators (13-week trends)
- Segmentation:
  - Consumer vs Commercial
  - Product type (credit cards, consumer loans, mortgages)
  - Currency split (TL vs FX)
- Regulatory impacts on growth patterns

**Example from Report:**
> "Loan growth maintains approximately 28% momentum via 13-week trend analysis. Consumer credit outpaces commercial credit growth, particularly through non-capped segments."

**Key Insight:** Use moving averages (13-week) rather than simple MoM for trend stability.

#### B. Funding & Liquidity
**Metrics Tracked:**
- Deposit growth rates
- Dollarization ratios (40% in report)
- Special schemes tracking (KKM: $7B threshold)
- Resident vs non-resident flows
- Fund flows

**Example from Report:**
> "The KKM scheme concluded in August, declining below $7 billion by September. Resident dollarization ratio, including funds, accelerates slightly above 40% but remains historically low."

**Key Insight:** Track special government schemes and provide historical context ("historically low").

#### C. Profitability Metrics
**Metrics Tracked:**
- Net Interest Margin (NIM) - quarterly trajectory
- Return on Equity (ROE) - annual projections
- Margin dynamics (credit rates vs deposit rates)
- Cost efficiency evolution

**Example from Report:**
> "Net Interest Margins (NIMs) expected to improve in Q4 2025. Return on Equity (ROE) forecast: approximately 26% for 2025 (revised upward from previous 25% estimate). 2026 ROE projection: 28-31% for deposit banks."

**Key Insights:**
- Show forecast revisions transparently
- Use ranges (28-31%) not point estimates
- Provide quarterly + annual views

#### D. Asset Quality
**Metrics Tracked:**
- NPL ratio trends
- Restructuring impacts
- Cost of Risk (basis points)
- Currency-adjusted metrics
- Coverage ratios

**Example from Report:**
> "Currency-adjusted cost of risk: nearly 150 basis points in 2025 (improved from previous 200bps forecast). Potential deterioration to approximately 200 basis points in 2026."

**Key Insight:** Currency-adjusted metrics essential for FX-sensitive markets.

## 3. METHODOLOGICAL APPROACHES

### Trend Analysis Techniques

**1. Moving Averages:**
- 13-week trends (vs volatile month-over-month)
- Smooths seasonality and noise
- Better for identifying true momentum

**2. Comparative Analysis:**
- Year-over-year (YoY)
- Quarter-over-quarter (QoQ)
- Historical benchmarks ("historically low/high")

**3. Segmentation:**
```
Geography:    Resident vs Non-resident
Product:      Consumer, Commercial, Credit Cards, Mortgages
Currency:     TL vs FX
Institution:  Deposit Banks, Participation Banks, etc.
```

### Forward-Looking Framework

**Time Horizons:**
- Near-term: Next quarter (Q4 2025)
- Medium-term: Full year (2025)
- Longer-term: Next year (2026)

**Forecast Methodology:**
- Range estimates: "28-31%" not "29.5%"
- Conditional projections: "contingent upon inflation outlook"
- Revision transparency: "revised upward from 25%"
- Uncertainty acknowledgment: "may delay into 2026"

**Example Structure:**
> "2026 ROE projection: 28-31% for deposit banks, contingent upon inflation outlook improvements and Central Bank rate flexibility"

This shows:
- Range (28-31%)
- Target group (deposit banks)
- Conditions (inflation, CB rates)

## 4. DATA PRESENTATION BEST PRACTICES

### Numerical Precision

**Use Specific Numbers:**
- Growth rates: "28%" not "high twenties"
- Thresholds: "$7 billion" not "several billion"
- Ratios: "40% dollarization" not "moderate dollarization"
- Basis points: "150 bps" for risk metrics

**When to Use Approximations:**
- "Approximately 26%" when forecast has ±0.5% uncertainty
- "Nearly 150 basis points" when rounding from 148-152 range
- "Above 40%" when trend is directional emphasis

### Contextual Framing

**Patterns to Follow:**

1. **Historical Context:**
   - "remains historically low"
   - "unprecedented levels"
   - "returned to pre-crisis levels"

2. **Directional Language:**
   - "accelerates slightly above"
   - "declining below"
   - "maintains momentum"

3. **Sequential Changes:**
   - "concluded in August"
   - "by September"
   - "expected to improve in Q4"

4. **Regulatory Milestones:**
   - "BRSA credit card and GPL restructuring decisions"
   - "growth restrictions"
   - "KKM scheme concluded"

## 5. NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

### Opening Statement Formula

**Pattern:** [Direction] + [Qualifier] + [Timing] + [Driver]

**Example:**
> "Financial conditions started to **turn supportive** [direction] **a bit** [qualifier] **in September** [timing] **with the fall in credit interest rates** [driver]."

**Components:**
- **Direction:** What changed (turn supportive, deteriorated, stabilized)
- **Qualifier:** Magnitude (significantly, modestly, slightly, a bit)
- **Timing:** When (in September, during Q3, year-to-date)
- **Driver:** Why (with the fall in rates, due to policy changes)

### Balanced Narrative Approach

Always present:
- **Positives:** "resilience in managing cost evolution"
- **Challenges:** "margins face pressure"
- **Opportunities:** "expanding loan volumes in higher-yield segments"

**Example Structure:**
```markdown
While [positive trend], [challenge] persists due to [cause].
However, [offsetting factor] provides [opportunity].
```

### Causality Chain

Show clear cause → effect relationships:

```
Central Bank rate cuts
  → Credit interest rates fall
    → Loan demand improves
      → But margins compress (deposit rates lag)
        → Near-term NIM pressure
          → Expected recovery in Q4 as deposit rates adjust
```

## 6. REGULATORY & MACRO INTEGRATION

### Explicit Policy Linkages

**Framework:**
```
Regulatory Change → Immediate Impact → Secondary Effects → Timing
```

**Example from Report:**
> "BRSA credit card and GPL restructuring decisions ease NPL pressure, though effects may delay into 2026"

**Components:**
- **Who:** BRSA (Banking Regulation and Supervision Agency)
- **What:** Credit card and GPL restructuring decisions
- **Impact:** Ease NPL pressure
- **Timing:** Effects may delay into 2026
- **Uncertainty:** "may delay" (not certain)

### Macro-Financial Linkages

**Essential Connections:**

1. **Monetary Policy → Banking:**
   - Central Bank rates → Lending/deposit rates → NIMs
   - Policy stance → Credit growth → Profitability

2. **Inflation → Banking:**
   - Inflation trajectory → Real returns → Deposit flows
   - CPI → Cost-of-risk adjustments

3. **FX Markets → Banking:**
   - Exchange rates → Dollarization → Funding mix
   - FX volatility → Asset quality (FX-indexed loans)

4. **Fiscal Policy → Banking:**
   - Government schemes (KKM) → Deposit competition
   - Regulations → Growth constraints

## 7. RISK & UNCERTAINTY HANDLING

### Transparent Uncertainty Language

**Timing Uncertainty:**
- "may delay into 2026" (not guaranteed)
- "expected to improve" (forecast, not fact)
- "potential deterioration" (scenario, not prediction)

**Conditional Forecasts:**
- "contingent upon inflation outlook improvements"
- "subject to Central Bank rate flexibility"
- "assuming no major policy shifts"

**Magnitude Uncertainty:**
- "approximately 26%" (±0.5%)
- "nearly 150 basis points" (145-152 range)
- "around $7 billion" (±$0.5B)

### Forecast Revision Protocol

**When revising, always show:**
1. **Old forecast:** "previous 25% estimate"
2. **New forecast:** "revised upward to approximately 26%"
3. **Direction:** "revised upward" (or downward)
4. **Reason (optional):** "due to stronger NIM recovery"

**Example:**
> "Return on Equity (ROE) forecast: approximately 26% for 2025 (revised upward from previous 25% estimate)"

### Risk Factor Presentation

**Structure:**
1. **Name the risk:** "Margin compression"
2. **Explain the mechanism:** "as deposit rate declines lag behind credit rate reductions"
3. **Quantify if possible:** "150→200 bps cost of risk"
4. **Time the impact:** "in 2026"

## 8. WHAT MAKES A REPORT PROFESSIONAL

### Specificity Checklist

✓ **Exact Metrics:**
- "28% loan growth" not "strong growth"
- "40% dollarization" not "high dollarization"
- "150 basis points" not "elevated cost of risk"

✓ **Named Entities:**
- "BRSA restructuring decisions" not "regulatory changes"
- "KKM scheme" not "deposit protection program"
- "13-week trend analysis" not "recent trends"

✓ **Precise Timing:**
- "concluded in August" not "recently ended"
- "by September" not "soon after"
- "Q4 2025" not "later this year"

### Analytical Depth Markers

✓ **Cause-Effect Chains:**
- Don't just state "margins improved"
- Explain: "margins improved as credit rate increases outpaced deposit rate adjustments in Q3"

✓ **Leading vs Lagging Indicators:**
- Leading: "Consumer credit acceleration signals improving confidence"
- Lagging: "NPL formation reflects prior period economic stress"

✓ **Micro-to-Macro Linkages:**
- Micro: "Credit card growth"
- Meso: "Consumer credit segment"
- Macro: "Inflation outlook, monetary policy"

### Credibility Signals

✓ **Historical Context:**
- "remains historically low"
- "highest level since 2019"
- "pre-pandemic average"

✓ **Methodology Transparency:**
- "via 13-week trend analysis"
- "currency-adjusted cost of risk"
- "excluding special schemes"

✓ **Uncertainty Acknowledgment:**
- "may delay"
- "approximately"
- "contingent upon"
- "potential"

✓ **Forecast Track Record:**
- Showing revisions builds trust
- "previous 25% estimate" → "revised to 26%"

### Actionability Features

✓ **Forward-Looking:**
- Not just "what happened" but "what's next"
- Multiple time horizons (Q4, FY25, 2026)

✓ **Scenario-Based:**
- Base case + alternatives
- Conditional projections
- "If X happens, expect Y"

✓ **Range Estimates:**
- "28-31% ROE" allows decision-making
- Better than single point "29.5%"

## 10. ESSENTIAL METRICS TO TRACK

### Growth Metrics (Monthly/Quarterly)
- **Loan Growth:**
  - Overall (13-week MA)
  - Consumer vs Commercial
  - TL vs FX
  - By product (credit cards, mortgages, consumer, SME, corporate)

- **Deposit Growth:**
  - Overall
  - TL vs FX
  - Time vs Demand
  - Resident vs Non-resident

### Profitability Metrics (Quarterly/Annual)
- **NIM:** Net Interest Margin (quarterly trend)
- **ROE:** Return on Equity (TTM and annual)
- **ROA:** Return on Assets
- **Cost-to-Income Ratio**
- **Fee Income Ratio**

### Risk Metrics (Monthly/Quarterly)
- **NPL Ratio:** Non-performing loans / Total loans
- **Cost of Risk:** Provisions / Average loans (in basis points, currency-adjusted)
- **Coverage Ratio:** Provisions / NPLs
- **Restructured Loans Ratio**
- **Stage 2 Loans Ratio** (IFRS 9)

### Liquidity Metrics (Monthly)
- **LDR:** Loan-to-Deposit Ratio
- **Dollarization Ratio** (including funds)
- **FX Position**
- **LCR:** Liquidity Coverage Ratio
- **NSFR:** Net Stable Funding Ratio

### Capital Metrics (Quarterly)
- **CAR:** Capital Adequacy Ratio
- **Tier 1 Ratio**
- **CET1 Ratio**
- **Leverage Ratio**

### Market Context (Daily/Monthly)
- **Policy Rate** (CBRT)
- **Inflation** (CPI, PPI)
- **USD/TRY Exchange Rate**
- **Government Bond Yields**
- **Special Scheme Balances** (KKM, etc.)

- "decelerates," "loses momentum," "weakens"

## 13. EXAMPLE NARRATIVE SNIPPETS

### Opening Paragraph
> "Turkish banking sector conditions **turned supportive** in November 2025 as **credit interest rates declined** by an average 150 basis points following the Central Bank's policy rate cuts. Loan growth **maintained strong momentum** at 28% year-over-year via 13-week trend analysis, with **consumer credit outpacing commercial lending**. However, **net interest margins faced pressure** as deposit rate adjustments **lagged behind** credit rate reductions, creating near-term profitability headwinds that are **expected to ease** by Q1 2026."

**Elements Used:**
- ✓ Directional: "turned supportive"
- ✓ Magnitude: 150 bps, 28%
- ✓ Timing: November 2025, Q1 2026
- ✓ Causality: "following the Central Bank's policy rate cuts"
- ✓ Balanced: Positive trend + challenge + outlook
- ✓ Specificity: 13-week trend analysis

### Forecast Section
> "For full-year 2025, we **revise upward** our ROE forecast to **approximately 26%** (from previous 25% estimate) reflecting **stronger-than-expected** cost management and **moderate asset quality performance**. Looking ahead to 2026, we project ROE in the **28-31% range** for deposit banks, **contingent upon** continued inflation deceleration and Central Bank rate flexibility. Downside risks include **potential margin compression** if deposit competition intensifies, which could pressure the forecast toward the **lower end of the range**."

**Elements Used:**
- ✓ Revision transparency: "revise upward from 25%"
- ✓ Approximation: "approximately 26%"
- ✓ Range forecast: "28-31% range"
- ✓ Conditions: "contingent upon"
- ✓ Risk factors: "Downside risks include"
- ✓ Scenario language: "lower end of the range"

### Risk Discussion
> "Asset quality metrics **remain resilient** but face **emerging headwinds** in 2026. While BRSA's credit card restructuring decisions **provide near-term relief**, their effects **may delay into Q2 2026** due to implementation timelines. We **maintain** our currency-adjusted cost of risk forecast at **approximately 150 basis points** for 2025, but flag **potential deterioration to around 200 basis points** in 2026 if economic growth disappoints or unemployment rises above 10%."

**Elements Used:**
- ✓ Balanced view: "resilient" + "emerging headwinds"
- ✓ Timing precision: "Q2 2026," "implementation timelines"
- ✓ Uncertainty: "may delay"
- ✓ Conditional risk: "if economic growth disappoints"

## 14. COMPARISON: AMATEUR vs PROFESSIONAL REPORTS

### Example: Loan Growth Analysis

**❌ Amateur Approach:**
> "Loans increased this month. Banks gave more loans to consumers. Credit cards were popular."

**Problems:**
- No specific numbers
- No timeframe clarity
- No context or drivers
- No segmentation depth
- No forward view

**✅ Professional Approach:**
> "Loan growth maintained robust 28% year-over-year momentum via 13-week trend analysis in November 2025, with consumer credit (34% YoY) significantly outpacing commercial lending (22% YoY). Credit card balances surged 42%, driven by regulatory cap removals and strong retail spending ahead of year-end. We expect this divergence to narrow in Q1 2026 as corporate investment picks up following improved business confidence indices."

**Strengths:**
- Specific metrics (28%, 34%, 22%, 42%)
- Methodology noted (13-week trend)
- Timeframe clear (Nov 2025)
- Segmentation (consumer vs commercial)
- Drivers explained (cap removals, retail spending)
- Forward-looking (Q1 2026 expectation)

## CONCLUSION

Professional banking sector reports are distinguished by:
- **Specificity** over generality
- **Context** over raw numbers
- **Causality** over mere correlation
- **Ranges** over point estimates
- **Transparency** over certainty
- **Forward-looking** over backward-looking
- **Actionability** over pure description

The BBVA report demonstrates that excellence comes from:
1. Rigorous analytical frameworks (four-pillar approach)
2. Methodological transparency (13-week trends, currency-adjustment)
3. Balanced narratives (positives, challenges, risks)
4. Clear temporal structure (current → near-term → medium-term)
5. Explicit uncertainty handling (conditional forecasts, ranges)

Your BDDK + EVDS data infrastructure provides all necessary raw materials. The next phase is building the analytical and narrative layers that transform data into insights.
